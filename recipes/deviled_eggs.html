Deviled Eggs

Description

Deviled eggs are a popular dish originating from ancient Rome and consist of peeled hard-boiled eggs cut in half with the yolk scooped out and refilled with other ingredients such as mayonnaise, mustard, and spices. They are best served cold.

The name "deviled" eggs comes from the usage of the word in English in the 18th century to refer to highly seasoned dishes.

Ingredients

  • Eggs
  • Mayonnaise
  • Sugar
  • Vinegar
  • Mustard
  • Vegetables
  • Seasonings

Steps

  1. Cut the boiled eggs in half and remove the yolks. Reserve the whites.
  2. Mash the yolks with a fork and add the remaining ingredients.
  3. Stuff or pipe the filling into the egg white halves.
